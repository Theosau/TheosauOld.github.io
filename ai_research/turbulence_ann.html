Turbulence Modelling with Artificial Neural Networks

During my masters in Mechanical at Imperial College London, I explored the potential of applying artificial neural networks (ANNs) for turbulence modelling under the supervision of Dr. Salvador Navarro-Martinez in parallel with a classmate, Alvaro Prat Balasch.

Find our publication in the International Journal of Computational Fluid Dynamics for a more in-depth technical description of the problem and our approach.


Summary

Turbulence in fluid dynamics, described as “the most important unsolved problem of classical physics” by physicist Richard Feynman in 1964 -who’s quote is still relevant today- is a particular case of a greater concept called deterministic chaos, and arises a wide range of fluid flows: from flows around cars and aeroplanes as well as greater geophysical scales such as ocean currents or atmospheric flows.

These flows are described by the partial differential Navier-Stokes equations. These equation can be solved numerically for specific types of flows, but are computationally expensive and unviable for highly turbulent flows.

Hence, a method called Large Eddy Simulations (LES) spatially averages the Navier-Stokes equation to resolve only the large scales of motion of the flows. Doing so gives rise to an additional stress component which must be modelled as it incorporates small-scale terms of motion.


The spatially averaged Navier-Stokes. The terms correspond to the usual meanings in the field of Fluid Dynamics which will not be discussed here. The term circled in red is the residual stress term which must be modelled to predict the fluids' motion.


We model this residual stress term using ANNs and features from the large scales of motions, such as the flow velocity in space or the velocity gradient in space. The residual stress term being non-linear, the ability of ANNs to model non-linear functions suggested its potential in closing the filtered Navier-Stokes Equations.

We obtained correlation coefficeints superior to various classical turbulence models, using similar features, for different types of turbulent flows, ranging from Homegenous Isotropic Turbulence or magnetohydrodyanmics flows.

Example of the predictions of a residual stress term on a plane of a buoyancy mixing turbulent flow. A correlation coefficient of 0.92 between predicted and exact terms for such flows. The model manages to learn a model to predict the small scale residual stress term from large scales of motion features.
