Research and Projects

My journey in artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) started in early 2018 during my masters in Mechanical Engineering at Imperial College London. To dive deeper into the subject and discover some of its business applications I undertook that summer a 4-month internship at , a company developing a cutting-edge software technology for Supply Chain Automation.

My research interests focus on two points: 1) the ability of empowering agents to reason in a compositional and human interpretable fashion, 2) how to integrate ML in physics problems. Hence, I explored the potential of ANNs in turbulence modelling in the field fluid dynamics during my Mechanical Engineering thesis. I then studied a masters in AI and ML at Imperial College London where I focused on neuro-symbolic AI in the scope of my MSc thesis to build a model with human interpretable reasoning abilities. My journey in fluid dynamics began when I started studying Mechanical Engineering at Imperial College London. A project I particularly enjoyed in the field of Design Engineering was the development of a binoic hand for below elbow amputees, specifically designed for digital interface use. I had the chance to work with 4 of my classmates and Mr. Alex Lewis, who works as an interior designer.

Our work served as a successful proof-of-concept of the design, make and test of a cheap, light-weight bionic hand. It served as a basis for next generations of students to build on.
